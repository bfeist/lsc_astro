## **Introduction**

67475 was sampled as a large dark clast on the top of the White Boulder on the rim of North Ray Crater, Apollo 16 (figures 1 and 9). It is similar to other smaller clasts in 67455 from the matrix of the boulder. 67475 contains unusual clasts, some with abundant melt, some with abundant Fe-rich olivine and ferro-augite. There are zap pits on the outer surfaces.

#### **Petrography**

67475 is a tough purplish-grey, glassy breccia (figures 4 and 6). Minkin et al. (1974) describe the matrix as *"a fragment-laden melt containing two major clast* 

![](_page_1_Figure_6.jpeg)

*Figure 5: Composition of some mafic minerals in 67475 as gleaned from Minkin et al. (1977).* 

*types – a dark clast rich in metallic iron and a light clast with a high percentage of olivine".* Minkin et al. (1974) present a table of mineral and clast types.

Minkin et al. (1974) and Stoffler et al. (1985) find that 67475 contains "unusual" clasts. Clasts of devitrified glass are present. Other clasts are rich in metallic iron. One clast type has Fe-rich olivine in abundance. Pyroxene chemistry is also unusual, with some ferroaugite reported (figure 5).

#### **Radiogenic age dating**

None

### **Cosmogenic isotopes and exposure ages**

Clark and Keith (1973) determined the cosmic-rayinduced activity of 23Na = 38 dpm/kg, 26Al = 126 dpm/ kg and 54Mn = 2 dpm/kg.

## **Processing**

A slab was cut through the middle of 67475 (figure 8). There are 21 thin sections.
