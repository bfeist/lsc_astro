### Introduction

67605 is a relatively large friable, white particle picked out of the soil sample from the rim of North Ray Crater – see section on 67601. It is similar to the White Boulder samples (67455 etc) – but it is about the size, shape and color of a golf ball.

# **Petrography**

Ryder and Norman (1980) have provided the only description: "67605 is a fragmental breccia with many plagioclase and plagioclase-rich breccia clasts, as well as opaque aphanitic impact melt debris which gives some area of the thin section a dark aspect" (figure 2). The also describe a small clast of mare basalt in this rock! Mineral chemistry is not available.

# **Chemistry**

Warren and Wasson (1978) provided the Curator with two analyses (table, figure 3). The sample has trace Ni, Ir and Au.
