# **68519**

## Glass-coated Impact Melt Breccia 10.56 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of 68519. Scale in cm/mm. S72-49569* 

#### **Introduction**

68519 is a rake sample collected from station 8 soil in an area thought of have disturbance from South Ray Crater – see section on 68501. This small lithic fragment had a glass coat that has been partially chipped away by micrometeorite bombardment.

#### **Petrography**

Ryder and Norman (1980) described 68519 as a "clastrich impact melt". The matrix consists of about 75% plagioclase laths, less that 150 microns, with interstitial mafic minerals. Opaque phases are small and not welldeveloped and include armalcolite, Fe-metal and troilite. Hunter and Taylor (1981) looked for rust. Numerous inclusions of plagioclase indicate that the protolith was a breccia (figure 2).

Compositional variation of Apollo 16 impact-melt rocks is discussed by Korotev (1994).

#### **Chemistry**

68519 appears to have a high Mg/Fe ratio.

### **Radiogenic age dating**

Norman et al. (2006) attempted to data 68519, but found that there was no Ar plateau. Age may be about 3.9 b.y., but who knows?
