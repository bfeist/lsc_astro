# **64559**

## Impact Melt Breccia 21.8 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of 64559. Cm/mm scale. S72-55387.* 

#### **Introduction**

64559 is a rake sample from Stone Mountain – see section on 64501. It appears to be a piece of the dark lithology that is part of the abundant dimict breccias from that location (64535 etc).

#### **Petrography**

The texture of 64559 is that of a basalt, but since it includes clasts of anorthite, it is an impact melt breccias (figure 2)

#### **Chemistry**

The composition of 64559 is similar to that of dark lithology of the dilithologic breccias from station 4 (table). It is trace element rich. Most important is that the Ni, Ir and Au are high indicating that it is an impact melt rock.

### **Other Studies**

Pearce and Simonds 1974) studied the magnetic properties. Gooley et al. (1973) reported the Ni and Co in the metallic iron.

#### **Processing**

There is only one thin section.
