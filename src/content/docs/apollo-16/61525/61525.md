# **Introduction**

61525 - 61529 were collected as part of a rake sample from near Plum Crater (figure 2) – see section on 61500. As sorted and described by Phinney et al. (1973) they may all be the same material. Only 61525 has been studied.

# **Petrography**

Warner et al. (1973) termed 61525 "glassy breccia" and Phinney et al. (1976) termed it "polymict". Phinney et al. (1976) reported lots of glass, but that glass "filaments" were lacking. It is highly fractured. McKay et al. (1986) and Joy et al. (2012) reported the maturity index Is /FeO = 3 (immature) for 61525.

Gooley et al. (1973) reported about 6 % Ni in metallic iron grains, and Floran et al. (1976) reported 190 ppm Ni total. Pearce and Simonds (1974) studied magnetic properties.

## **Chemistry**

McKay et al. (1986) reported the composition which is like that of the soil (figure 4).

## **Other Studies**

The rare gas content was reported by McKay et al. (1986) who found excess <sup>40</sup>Ar and concluded it was an ancient regolith breccia (about 2.3 b.y. old).
