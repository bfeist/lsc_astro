# **12061**

## Ilmenite Basalt 9.5 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: PET photo of 12061 showing several pieces. Note center piece has large zap pit. No scale. NASA # S69-61659.* 

#### **Introduction**

12061 is an ilmenite basalt with medium-grained ophitic to subophitc texture with high percentage of pyroxene. It has not been dated.

#### **Petrography**

Neal et al. (1994) show a picture of the texture of 12061 (figure 2) and give mineral analyses. In an appendix to their paper, they describe some olivine phenocrysts (Fo66) as cores to pyroxene phenocrysts (<1.8 mm). Groundmass includes laths of plagioclase (1 mm), pyroxene, ilmenite, tridymite, glass with minute anhedral ulvÃ¶spinel, troilite and metal.

#### **Mineralogy**

*Olivine:* Olivine with a wide range of composition Fo is found as cores to pyroxene phenocrysts. 66-30

*Pyroxene:* The pyroxene quadrilateral is shown in figure 3.

*Plagioclase:* Plagioclase laths are An91-87.

*Metal:* The metal grains in 12061 were analyzed by Neal et al. (1994) (figure 4).

#### **Chemistry**

The chemical composition of 12061 is given in table 1 and figure 5 and 6.
