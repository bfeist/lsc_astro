# **15529**

# Vesicular Mare Basalt 1531 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of 15529. Sample is 10 cm across (cube is 1 inch). NASA S87-48191.* 

Lunar sample 15529 is a very vesicular mare basalt 15529 is probably related to the olivine-normative clan (figure 1) that remains unstudied. It was collected from of Apollo 15 basalts. The mineral mode reported in near the edge of Hadley Rille in an area called "The the original catalog seems too high in plagioclase Terrace". The lunar regolith was thin in this area, with content. abundant rock samples (basalts) exposed (Swann et al. 1971).

**Mineralogical Mode for 15529** Dirt fills some vesicles, but not all, and the sample has **Sample Catalog** no obvious zap pits. **Butler 1971**
