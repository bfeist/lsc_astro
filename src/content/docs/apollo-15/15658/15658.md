# **15658**

# Olivine-normative Basalt 11.6 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of 15658. Scale is 1 cm. S71-49534.* 

### **Introduction**

15658 is a rake sample collected from the Terrace at Hadley Rille (station 9a, Apollo 15). It is typical of most of the other rake samples from this location (see section on 15614).

## **Petrography**

Ryder (1985) provides the only description: "15658 is a medium-grained, olivine-bearing basalt (figure 2). Pigeonites range from 1 to 2 mm long and are twinned and zoned. Most plagioclases for stubby crystals up to about 1 mm, some of which are hollow. Some radial growth of plagioclase and pyroxene is present. Olivine forms scattered anhedral phenocrysts, and smaller grains are present, many as inclusions in pigeonite. Cristobalite, fayalite, and a range of opaque phases are present." No mode is given.

### **Chemistry**

Helmke et al. (1973) and Chappell et al. (1973) reported analyses of 15658. It is essentially the same as that of 15555.

### **Other Studies**

Gose et al. (1972) and Pearce et al. (1978) determined the magnetic properties.

### **Processing**

There are only 2 thin sections of 15658.
