### **Introduction**

Large lunar breccia 15505 is from the area near Hadley Rrille were a large field of mare basalts were found (figure 3). It is similar to 15558 â€“ from same location. It is a glass matrix breccia with an abundance for mare basalt clasts (figures 1 - 7).

![](_page_0_Picture_7.jpeg)

*Figure 3: Map of Apollo 15 showing location of 15505.* 

### **Petrography**

Fruland (1985), Simon et al. (1986) and McKay et al. (1989) compared 15505 with other Apollo 15 soil breccias. McKay et al. reported that the maturity index for 15505 was Is /FeO = 26 and Simon et al. reported the mode.

![](_page_1_Picture_0.jpeg)

*Figure 4: Photos of 15505. 71-47364* 

Wilshire and Moore (1974) and Michel-Levy and Jahann (1973) studied the glass coating, finding that it was continuous with the interior veins and probably not a glass splash.

Engelhardt et al. (1973) found that 15505 was made up of primarily mare basalt components. A single large clast of mare basalt was exposed in the slab, but has apparently not been studied.

### **Chemistry**

15505 is one of the most iron-rich breccias from Apollo 15 (figure 11). It has a REE pattern like that of soil from the Apollo 15 site (figure 12).

Moore et al. (1973) reported 104 ppm carbon (figure 6).

Winzer et al. (1978) reported the composition of the glass coat (but it is surely not homogeneous).
