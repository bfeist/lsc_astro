# **Introduction**

79175 is a polymict breccia with fragments of regolith breccia cemented together in a glassy matrix – something like a giant agglutinate.

# **Petrography**

Fruland (1983) and Simon et al. (1990) included 79175 in their study of regolith breccias. They found that it was 66 % matrix

Neal and Taylor (1993) reported that the cementing glass was "ropy" and that "it permeates the rock, welding clasts of soil breccias, basalt (coarse and fine grained), and other clasts into a coherent mass. The glass varies from fresh and vitreous to dull and aphanitic".

There are no studies of this sample to be found in the literature.

### *Significant clasts*

Neal and Taylor (1993) reported on three basalt clasts.

# **Chemistry**

Simon et al (1990) reported the only analysis.
