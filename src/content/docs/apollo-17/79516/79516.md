### **Introduction**

79516 is a small basalt sample collected by raking near Van Serg Crater out in the middle of Taurus-Littrow Valley.

### **Petrography**

Neal and Taylor (1993) are the only ones to study the petrography of 79516. They describe the texture as subophitc to ophitic. However, it has a quench texture with olivine and ilmenite-armaclolite phenocrysts set in a very fine-grained matrix of pink pyroxene, plagioclase, ilmenite and opaque glass (figure 3).

### **Chemistry**

Warner et al. (1979) determined the chemical composition. It is akin to type B Apollo 17 basalt.

### **Processing**

There are two thin sections.
