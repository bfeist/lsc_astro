# **76136** Ilmenite Basalt 86.6 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_1.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photos of 76135. Cube is 1 cm. S73-23923 and 934.* 

# **Introduction**

76136 is a rare example of a mare basalt that has been transported across the mare-highland boundary at the base of the North Massif, Apollo 17. Its surface is rounded by micrometeorite bombardment (figure 1). It is an ilmenite basalt with olivine phenocrysts.

# **Petrography**

Brown et al. (1975) give the modal mineralogy of 76136 and other Apollo 17 basalts. Elongate ilmenite and equant olivine are set in a holocrystalline intersertal matrix (figure 2). Olivine is surrounded by pyroxene. Pyroxene chemistry has not been studied.

Usselman et al. (1975) experimentally reproduced textures and mineral chemistries of high-titanium mare basalts.

# **Chemistry**

The chemical composition of 76136 was determined by Rhodes et al. (1976). It is a type A, Apollo 17 basalt (figures 4 and 5).

# **Radiogenic age dating**

Nyquist et al. (1976) determined Rb, Sr and Sr87/86, but did not determine an age.
