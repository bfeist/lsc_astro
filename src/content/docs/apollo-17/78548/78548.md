## **Introduction**

78548 and 78549 are very friable breccias, that appear to be the equivalent of the local soil where they were collected. They broke up into fines during processing (figures 4 a, b). These particle should be studied in direct comparison to the soil (78501).

# **Petrography**

Meyer (1994), Keil et al. (1974) and Warner et al. (1978) included these particles in their catalogs. Both fragments contained small clasts of mare basalt, feldspathic breccias and other soil components. 78548 was found to have pale green glass, while78549 included agglutinates.
