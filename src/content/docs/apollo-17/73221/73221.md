# **Introduction**

Station 3 was on the ejecta blanket of Lara Crater (Wolfe et al. 1981). Breccia samples 73235 and 73255 were located nearby as was a double drive tube (unopened).

The station 3 trench was dug in an area on the rim of a ten meter crater where there was noticeable color variation (figure 3). The nicely persevered color variation indicates that micro-gardening has not recently disturbed the regolith at this site. The color

variation is due to variation in agglutinate content and not due to a variation in chemical composition.

This trench showed a nice progression of cosmic-rayinduced activity – from high at the surface and low at depth - showing that the activity was due to low energy solar flares rather than high energy cosmic rays (figure 1). The only problem with trenching is that there is slumping from surface material, preventing perfect sampling.

## **Petrography**

The maturity of 73221, 73241, 73261 and 73281 is I/ FeO = 43, 18, 45 and 34 and the average grain size is 70, 81, 60 and 60 microns, respectively (Morris 1978, Graf 1993).

![](_page_1_Figure_7.jpeg)

Figure 5: Carbon content and maturity index for two trench samples.

Meyer (1973) found an abundance of feldspathic breccia in the 4-10 mm size fraction of 73244. Jolliff et al. (1996) and Bence et al. (1974) studied numerous coarse-fine particles from 73243 and 73263 respectively.
