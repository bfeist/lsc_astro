Soil 413 grams

### **Introduction**

79511 was collected from the rim of Van Serg Crater, about 2 meters from soil sample 79211 and breccia sample 79135 (figure 1). Several lines of evidence indicate that Van Serg Crater is young, but that it sampled old mare regolith (Wolfe et al. 1981). The soil is similar to the regolith breccias from this location, pieces of which were returned in the same bag.

## **Petrography**

The maturity index of 79511 is Is /FeO = 61 and the average grain size is 107 microns (Morris 1978, Graf 1993). The grain size distribution was determined by Butler and King (1974) and von Engelhardt et al. (1976) (figure 6). Coarse-fine particles were cataloged by Meyer (1973).

### **Chemistry**

The bulk chemical composition of 79511 was determined by Mason et al. (1974) and Laul et al. (1974). Trace element analysis by isotope dilution mass spectrometry (Philpotts et al. 1974) are consistent with those by instrumental neutron activation analysis (Laul et al. 1974; Korotev and Kremser 1992). 79511 is a mare soil (figure 4 and 5), but this sample may have an added component of crushed dark matrix breccia from the 11 friable pieces returned in same bag (79517- 79537).

### **Cosmogenic isotopes and exposure ages**

79511 has been exposed to a high flux of neutrons (Curtis and Wasserburg 1977) and a high radiation dose of solar-cosmic-rays (Goswami and Lal 1974). This is seemingly inconsistent with the apparent young age of Van Serg Crater and indicates a pre-crater exposure to space radiation.
