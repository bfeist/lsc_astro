# **74245** Aphanitic Ilmenite Basalt 63.34 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_1.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of 74245. S73-17966.Sample is 5 cm long* 

### **Introduction**

74245 was found in the soil sample (74240) collected from the end of the trench dug at Shorty Crater (see section on 74220). It is an aphanitic basalt.

## **Petrography**

Brown et al. (1975) reported the mineral mode for 74245 finding a high percentage of "opaque" glass. Small phenocrysts of olivine are Fo79. Phenocrysts of armalcolite are present. The overall texture is that of a basaltic vitrophyre (figure 4)

Usselman et al. (1975) determined the cooling rate (15 25 deg./hr.).

### **Chemistry**

Neal (2001), Rhodes et al. (1975) and Warner et al. (1975) all reported chemical analyses. The Rb content is relatively high (type C?).

### **Radiogenic age dating**

Apollo 17 mare basalts are generally considered 3.72 ± 0.04 b.y. old (see Paces et al. 1991). Nyquist et al. (1975) determined Rb, Sr and Sr87/86.

### **Processing**

74245 was cut by band saw and there are 5 thin sections.
