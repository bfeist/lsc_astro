# **10091**

Breccia 24 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of remaining piece of 10091,26. Scale is in cm. NASA S76-25548.* 

#### **Introduction**

Karmer et al. (1977) give the weight of this breccia sample as 24 grams and explain that part of this sample was included in the "biopool" sample (10002). 10091,26 is the remaining piece (figure 1).

#### **Petrography**

10091 is a dark coherent breccia that breaks into angular pieces. It is probably a regolith breccia.

#### **Chemistry**

Oro et al. (1970) give an analysis of a "cleaved surface and an outer surface" of 10091 by "spark ionization mass spectroscopy."

### **Radiogenic age dating**

No data.

## **FProcessing**

About half of this sample was included in the "biopool". Apollo 11 samples were originally described and cataloged in 1969 and "recataloged" by Kramer et al. (1977). There are no petrographic thin sections for 10091.
