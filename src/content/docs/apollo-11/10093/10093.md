# **10093**

## Regolith Breccia 25.8 grams

![](_page_0_Picture_2.jpeg)

*Figure 1: Photo of 10093,0. NASA S76-25989. Sample is about 5 cm.* 

#### **Introduction**

10093 is a typical Apollo 11 regolith breccias. It is relatively coherent, with typical dark glass matrix and sparce lithic clasts (figure 1). It has micrometeorite "zap" pits on the surface.

#### **Petrography**

Simon et al. (1984) included breccia 10093 in their comprehensive study of Apollo 11 regolith breccias â€“ their mode is given in the table. It has a high proportion of matrix (figure 3). They calculated that it had about 24 % highland component, but couldn't directly identify that many clasts of highland rock.

#### **Chemistry**

Rhodes and Blanchard (1981) found that the composition of 10093 was similar to the other regolith breccias and 10084 (figures 4 and 5).
